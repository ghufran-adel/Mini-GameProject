Game Overview:

Hangman is a word puzzle game where one player thinks of a word, phrase, or a specific set of letters, and the other player (or players) attempts to guess the word or phrase by suggesting letters. The game is often used as a fun way to test vocabulary and word-guessing skills.

How to Play:

Setting Up:

One player (the "word thinker") thinks of a word or phrase and keeps it secret from the other player(s). The word or phrase is typically represented by a series of blank spaces, one for each letter.

Guessing Letters:

The other player(s) take turns guessing letters. If the guessed letter is part of the word or phrase, it is revealed in its correct position(s). If the guessed letter is not part of the word, a part of the hangman (usually drawn in a stepwise manner) is added to represent incorrect guesses. The hangman is usually represented as a stick figure being "hanged."

Winning:

The guessing player(s) win if they successfully guess the word or phrase before the hangman is fully drawn. The word thinker can reveal the word or phrase at the end of the game.

Limited Attempts:

The guessing player(s) have a limited number of attempts (usually represented by the number of parts of the hangman). If they exceed this limit without guessing the word, they lose the game.
